Notes / Explanation

I started with the animation of Satan and Mr. Cabal. Now you may have guessed that these are not models of the characters from the book I happen to be reading at the moment, because those don't exist. I instead chose to use models of characters they reminded me of, replacing Mr Cabal with a character known as Spamton and Satan one referred to as Tenna (like antenna, because he has a CRT for a head). From this point forward I'll be referring to them by those names for simplicity's sake.

I have three primary reasons behind my choice for the character models. The first of course has its roots in personal interest. I had already seen and was already curious about the models from familiarity with the characters. The second is my desire for practice with complex and simple rigs. I've used the rig generator used on Tenna before, so I knew the basics of the controls, but the topology and weight painting were poor and I wanted practice with properly made models, hoping it would help me understand rigging better. I also wanted to practice animating simpler rigs, so I chose the Spamton model for that purpose. The third reason is that these characters don't need lip flaps, and thats going to be a requirement if I'm working with dialogue and I want to submit this on time.

I did have to make a few modifications to the rigs though. For Tenna, I had to redo the shading, as it was much too stylized, though I kept it for the face, as it seemed fitting for a glowing screen. Spamton also needed a minor shader adjustment to add transparency to his glasses for one shot, but the primary issue was in the rigging. The initial issue being that the eye controls weren't hooked up properly so I had to learn what a "Damped Track" was, but I also found that there was no bone to control the position of the glasses; It seems they were meant to stay in place. While not necessarily a rigging issue, Tenna doesn't have any controls to change the shape of his head, so I used a lattice modifier in the last shot, as I felt it wasn't expressive enough.

You may notice that I cut the shot where Tenna points his thumb at himself. My plans had to change when I realized that the rigs arm poles weren't working properly so I couldn't position the elbows. I had already animated up to that point so I had to cut to the next shot to avoid having to redo the animation. I ended up just importing a new version of the rig, this time giving a thorough test for every function I planned on using.

While animating to an audio recording ensured I would hit the required length, after test screenings with my family I figured a short prelude to ease the viewer in would be best, so I cut the last board and started working on the opening shot where the narrator uses her dress to drift down and softly land on the couch; I made her myself with rigify. I certainly appreciated finishing with one of my own models, while I felt the limitations of the poor weight painting I think I was able to navigate it well.
